Mississippi's oldest synagogue attacked with fire.

By Christine Shin
Source: Beth Israel Congregation

Mississippi officials arrested suspect Stephen Spencer Pittman Saturday and charged him with arson after a fire was set early that morning at Beth Israel, Jacksonâ€™s only synagogue.

The 19-year-old was taken into custody Saturday evening at a nearby hospital, where he was being treated for non-life-threatening burns, according to officials.

The fire broke out around 3 a.m. No injuries were reported.

Officials said the fire caused extensive damage to the building, including:

  • The synagogue library, where two Torahs were destroyed
  • Significant smoke damage throughout the building
  • The main sanctuary, where five Torahs were damaged

Beth Israel has previously been targeted. Decades ago, the synagogue was bombed by the Ku Klux Klan while the congregation was advocating for racial integration.

Congregation President Zach Shemper said rebuilding could take between one and a half and three years. In the meantime, several Jackson houses of worship, including Northminster Baptist Church, have offered space for services.

Congregation leaders said they expect to resume services as early as next week and will continue sharing updates during the rebuilding process.
